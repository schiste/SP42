## Scope boundary

This PRD characterizes **what a score, a signal, and a rank MEAN to the operator** — the user-facing semantics of how SP42 decides what to look at first, why an edit surfaced where it did, and what action might fit. Scoring/ranking semantics are themselves a PRD trigger (`docs/prd/README.md`). It deliberately excludes two adjacent concerns:

- **How the score is computed** — the signal catalogue and its weights, the base/accumulate/clamp arithmetic, the composite-score and signal-contribution internal types, the priority-queue mechanism, the queue-policy override pass, and the way a human-readable policy file is parsed and compiled into the runtime config — is *implementation*, governed by the scoring charter (`docs/scoring/SCORING_CONSTITUTION.md`) and, for the LiftWing input, ADR-0001 §9. This PRD references those mechanisms; it does not specify them. (There is no ADR for the scoring *contract* itself yet — tracked in #19.)
- **What the operator does with the ranked queue** — opening the live view, walking it one revision at a time, and choosing a disposition — is the review *workflow*, owned by **PRD-0002**. What a chosen disposition then does on the wiki is owned by **PRD-0004**. This PRD owns only the meaning of the numbers and reasons that drive those decisions, including the score-gated *suggestion* of a disposition.

## Problem

A reviewer working a recent-changes patrol queue faces far more incoming revisions than they can read. Without a score and a rank, every edit looks equally urgent: the operator has no principled way to decide *what to look at first*, *why it surfaced*, or *what action might fit*. They are forced to triage by hand, in arrival order, and the genuinely harmful changes compete for attention with routine maintenance edits.

This PRD characterizes what SP42 already ships to solve that: a **deterministic local scoring engine** that turns each edit into a single composite score with a fully decomposed list of reasons, an **optional LiftWing damage-probability signal** folded in as one supporting input among many, and a **priority-ranked queue** that orders the highest-scoring (most review-worthy) edits first. It is built for an experienced patroller who needs the queue to be *interpretable and trustworthy*, not a black box.

A second, governance-level concern is baked into the shipped semantics: the *edited revision's author* identity (anonymous IP, temporary account) is predictive of risk but must never *be* the verdict. The operator should understand that this identity acts only as a **bounded, visible, explainable modifier** — capped so it can never dominate a rank by itself. *(This is about the author of the edit being ranked; the **operator's** own identity and what they may do is a separate concern, **PRD-0005**.)* The constitutional basis for the cap, the fairness stance, and the policy-over-hidden-tuning promise is the scoring charter (`docs/scoring/SCORING_CONSTITUTION.md` §7, §8, §10).

## Proposal

What the shipped feature lets an operator understand and do. *(How each is computed is implementation / the scoring charter; this section is the meaning.)*

- **Read one composite score per edit.** Each queued revision carries a single integer score in a configured `0..max_score` band (default `0..100`). The operator reads it as one interpretable number: how review-worthy this edit is.
- **See *why* it scored that way.** The score is never opaque — it ships with an itemized list of reasons, each a named signal with a human-readable note. The families the operator will see span edit-shape cues (new page, large content removal, bot-like edit), comment/diff cues (profanity, link spam, mass blanking, inserted profanity, repeated-character noise), reverted-before and trusted-user tags, warning history, an obvious-vandalism fast lane, and duplicate patterns. *(The signal catalogue and its weights are implementation, tuned in policy — see the charter §6/§10.)*
- **Work the queue top-down.** Scored edits are ordered highest-score-first; ties break deterministically so the queue is stable across runs and the operator can trust their position in it.
- **Have a damage-probability ML signal folded in when available.** When a LiftWing `revertrisk`/`damaging` probability exists for the revision, it contributes one `LiftWing risk` signal — *supplementary, never required* (ADR-0001 §9; charter §9). A missing or invalid probability simply contributes nothing, and the local deterministic score still ranks the queue. The operator never has to wait on, or trust, an external service to use SP42.
- **Understand identity as a capped modifier, not a verdict.** Anonymous and temporary-account editors add a risk modifier *only when the active policy enables it*, and the combined identity contribution is clamped to a policy cap (±25 in the active French-Wikipedia policy). When the cap bites, the operator sees an explicit `Identity cap adjustment` line in the reasons. The same edit is assessed identically regardless of author identity once that cap is reached — identity can never solely dominate a rank. *(This is the user-facing fairness semantic; the cap mechanism is implementation, mandated by charter §7.)*
- **Trust that constructive maintenance sinks, not rises.** Edits that only add references, categories, interwiki links, or wikilink wrappers contribute *negative* weight, lowering patrol priority; trusted-user and bot-like edits carry strong negative weights. A good newcomer edit is pulled *down* the queue rather than buried by suspicion.
- **Suppress trusted editors and boost repeat-offender bursts at queue level.** A queue heuristic policy can name trusted usernames (those registered editors then receive the trusted-user reduction) and can boost newcomer-like duplicate clusters — many similar edits from different fresh accounts/IPs hitting the same page raise the rank of the whole cluster. The operator sees coordinated bursts rise together and known-good editors fall away.
- **Read the rank at a glance and trim the queue.** The patrol UI maps a score to a colour tier — red at ≥70, amber at ≥30, green below — so the operator scans severity visually. An operator-set minimum-score filter and a result limit trim the visible queue to what the operator wants to work.
- **Get a score-gated action suggestion.** The live-operator preflight *suggests* a disposition from the score and its signals: rollback when obvious-vandalism / duplicate-pattern signals fire or score ≥70; undo when a duplicate pattern fires or score ≥40 (unless trusted-user suppression is present); mark-patrolled only for clean, low-score, unpatrolled edits (score <60). The suggestion is **advisory** — the operator still chooses, and what the chosen disposition does on the wiki is **PRD-0004**. There is no autonomous action gated on score alone.
- **Rely on score semantics living in reviewable policy, not hidden constants.** Weights, thresholds, the identity cap, queue limits, the LiftWing contribution ceiling, and combination rules are declared in a human-readable, lifecycle-staged policy file (`active` / `candidate` / `suggested`) rather than baked into code, so the operator (or a reviewer auditing fairness) can read and evolve the scoring/ranking semantics as policy. *(The file schema and the compile-to-runtime step are implementation; the lifecycle and the policy-over-tuning promise are the charter, §10/§12 and `docs/scoring/POLICY_LAYOUT.md`.)*

## Definition of Done

Re-framed as characterization. Each item is an operator-observable behavior that is already true and bound to an existing test. *(The pure scoring **mechanism** — weight compilation, clamp arithmetic, policy parsing, queue internals — is additionally unit- and property-tested in `scoring_engine.rs`, `scoring_policy.rs`, `queue_builder.rs`, and `priority_queue.rs`; those mechanism tests back the scoring contract (no ADR yet — tracked in #19). The items below are the operator-facing outcomes.)*

- [x] An edit accumulates multiple weighted signals into one composite score with itemized reasons the operator can read — verified by `scoring_engine.rs::scores_multiple_positive_signals`.
- [x] A bot-like (maintenance-shaped) edit sinks via a strong negative weight that reduces the total — verified by `scoring_engine.rs::bot_signal_reduces_total`.
- [x] The score the operator reads always stays inside the configured `base_score..max_score` band, even under extreme weights — verified by `scoring_engine.rs::extreme_weights_do_not_overflow_total_or_scaling` and the property test `scoring_engine.rs::property_score_stays_within_config_bounds`.
- [x] The number shown equals the clamped sum of the reasons shown — explainability is faithful — verified by `scoring_engine.rs::property_total_matches_clamped_contribution_sum`.
- [x] Each reason appears at most once in the list — verified by `scoring_engine.rs::property_signals_are_emitted_at_most_once`.
- [x] Anonymous and temporary-account authors produce distinct, identity-specific reasons the operator can tell apart — verified by `scoring_engine.rs::scores_multiple_positive_signals` (anonymous) and `scoring_engine.rs::temporary_accounts_get_distinct_identity_signal`.
- [x] Combined identity contribution is clamped to the policy cap, emitting a visible `Identity cap adjustment` reason when it bites, so identity can never solely dominate the rank — verified by `scoring_engine.rs::applies_identity_cap_adjustment`.
- [x] A LiftWing probability, when present, is folded in as one supporting `LiftWing risk` reason — verified by `scoring_engine.rs::applies_warning_history_and_liftwing_context`.
- [x] A missing or non-finite LiftWing probability degrades silently to no signal rather than erroring the score — verified by `scoring_engine.rs::ignores_non_finite_liftwing_risk_values`, with the probability normalized to `0.0..=1.0` before use — `context_builder.rs::clamps_liftwing_probability_into_unit_interval`.
- [x] A LiftWing response is parsed into a unit-interval probability across its supported shapes, rejecting out-of-range values — verified by `liftwing.rs::parses_direct_probability_shape`, `liftwing.rs::parses_scores_revertrisk_shape`, and `liftwing.rs::rejects_probability_outside_unit_interval`.
- [x] The queue surfaces the highest-scoring edit first — verified by `queue_builder.rs::ranks_highest_score_first` and `priority_queue.rs::dequeues_highest_priority_first`.
- [x] Edits with equal scores keep a stable (insertion) order across runs — verified by `priority_queue.rs::preserves_insertion_order_for_equal_priorities`.
- [x] A named trusted user is suppressed at queue-build time, dropping below a risky anonymous edit — verified by `queue_builder.rs::trusted_user_policy_suppresses_registered_editor`.
- [x] Repeated newcomer-like edits to the same page rise together via a duplicate-cluster boost — verified by `queue_builder.rs::duplicate_cluster_boost_applies_to_newcomer_patterns`.
- [x] Contextual risk inputs (warning history, LiftWing) change the rank order the operator sees — verified by `queue_builder.rs::applies_contextual_scores_when_ranking`.
- [x] The active French-Wikipedia policy compiles, carrying the identity cap (25), the queue default limit (25), and its combination rules into the runtime — verified by `scoring_policy.rs::parses_and_compiles_scoring_policy` (asserts the identity cap, the default limit, and the combination rules); `scoring_policy.rs::loads_default_embedded_active_policy` separately confirms the embedded active policy loads as `frwiki`/`v0.1` with its identity cap.
- [x] The score maps to the operator-visible colour tiers at the 70 / 30 / 0 thresholds — verified by `components/style.rs::score_tier_maps_thresholds`.
- [x] A high-scoring edit yields a rollback suggestion in the live-operator preflight — verified by `live_operator.rs::preflight_recommends_rollback_for_high_score_edit`.

## Risks

*(Retroactive PRD — residual risks of the shipped behavior, with mitigations as
built; not a pre-implementation risk forecast. The code mechanisms behind them are
implementation / the scoring charter.)*

- **The operator over-trusts the rank and skips reading the diff.** *Mitigation:* the score is advisory and always decomposed into reasons the operator is meant to read; action recommendations are suggestions, not auto-actions. There is no autonomous action path gated on score alone.
- **Identity weighting unfairly buries good newcomer edits.** *Mitigation:* identity contributes only behind enable flags and a hard ±cap with a visible adjustment line; constructive-maintenance signals carry negative weight to pull good edits down the queue. *Residual risk:* the cap is a *policy* number — a misconfigured policy could raise it (see Known gaps on the missing fairness/ranking regression test wired to the live engine).
- **A LiftWing outage or malformed response degrades ranking quality silently.** *Mitigation:* LiftWing is optional and a missing/invalid probability contributes nothing rather than erroring the score; the local deterministic score still ranks the queue.
- **Weight tuning produces a runaway or negative total.** *Mitigation:* the total the operator sees is always clamped to the configured band and proven bounded under adversarial weights — verified by `scoring_engine.rs::property_extreme_config_weights_never_escape_score_bounds`.
- **The operator misreads a low score as "safe."** *Mitigation:* the colour tier and the obvious-vandalism / duplicate-pattern fast-lane suggestions surface high risk independently of a raw threshold.

## Known gaps / drift

Factual observations noticed while reverse-engineering; not design proposals.

- **`min_score_cutoff` policy field is not enforced at runtime.** The active policy and the compiled queue config carry `min_score_cutoff`, but no scoring or queue-build code consumes it. The only score-based queue trimming is the *operator-set* `min_score` UI filter, which is a separate, unrelated value. The policy cutoff is effectively documentation.
- **`account_age_modifier_enabled` is plumbed but unused.** It exists in policy and config (set `false`) but no scoring code reads it — there is no account-age signal in the engine. It is a reserved knob with no behaviour.
- **The LiftWing contribution clamp has no dedicated unit test.** The configured LiftWing weight is clamped to `±max_contribution`, and the active policy's weight (35) equals its `max_contribution` (35) so the clamp is a no-op in practice. No test exercises a configured weight *exceeding* the ceiling, so the clamp itself is uncovered.
- **Ranking and fairness eval fixtures parse but are not run against the live engine.** `evals/scoring/fixtures/vandalism_patrol/frwiki/ranking.yaml` and `fairness.yaml` declare ordering comparisons and per-cohort false-positive-regression checks, and the governance script asserts the files exist, but the only tests are *parse* tests (`scoring_evaluation.rs::parses_ranking_fixtures`, `::parses_fairness_fixtures`). No test feeds the declared ranking comparisons or fairness cohorts through the live scoring/ranking path to assert the engine actually satisfies them. The fairness invariant ("identity can never dominate") is enforced structurally by the cap, but not regression-tested against the corpus — the charter §11 evaluation baseline is therefore only partly realized.
- **The diff-derived risk hints are wired into per-selection rescore, but not into queue ranking.** The diff analyzer produces `link_addition_only` / `reference_addition_only` / `mass_blanking_detected` / etc. hints, and the engine consumes the corresponding context flags, but the shared context builder hard-codes every diff-derived flag to disabled and the batch queue-ranking pass uses no diff context — so at *ranking* time these diff-aware signals never fire. They *do* fire for the selected revision's detail rescore: the server's `load_selected_review_state` (`crates/sp42-server/src/live_queue.rs`) fetches the diff and calls `sp42_core::diff_engine::analyze_diff_for_scoring` to populate the flags before rescoring. So the ordering of the queue is still diff-blind even though the selected item's score is not. They are also exercised in unit tests (`scoring_engine.rs::applies_new_diff_aware_signals_from_context`).
- **Queue-level limits (`default_limit` / `max_limit`) are governance numbers, not runtime enforcement.** They compile into the queue defaults and are validated for sanity, but the live queue's result cap comes from the UI filter's `limit`, not from the compiled policy default. The two are not connected in code.
- **The trusted-user *tag* path and the trusted-user *username* path both feed the same reason but from different layers.** Tag-based trust is a per-edit signal in the scoring engine; username-based trust is a queue-policy override applied at ranking time. An operator reading "Trusted user" in the reasons cannot tell from the score alone which path fired without reading the note. This is a transparency nuance, not a correctness bug.
